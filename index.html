Who We are

Ahmed Firoz Kabir was born on 26 July 1963 in Sujanagar Upazila under Pabna district. Late Ahmed Tafiz Uddin and Ms. Firoza Begum are his parents. Firoza Begum was a dedicated school principal all her life. His father, Ahmed Tafiz Uddin, was a notable political personality in Pabna who stood by the political ideology of Bangabandhu Sheikh Majibur Rahman till the last day of his life. Ahmed Tafiz Uddin was twice elected as a member of parliament. He was also elected as the Upazila Chairman of Sujanagar.

Tafiz Uddin made a critical contribution to the Bangladesh Awami League. He was the President of the Sujanagar Awami League and Vice President of the Pabna Awami League. Most importantly, Tafiz Uddin was a fearless freedom fighter in the War of Independence. He was the freedom fighters' camp in-charge in Kechuadanga, India.


Ahmed Firoz Kabir passed his SSC examination from Satbaria High School and HSC from Rajshahi College. Firoz Kabir completed his Bachelor's degree in Finance from the University of Rajshahi. Subsequently, he earned his Master's degree in Finance from the University of Dhaka.

Ahmed Tafiz Uddin died on 28 June 1998 while he was a running member of parliament. During the 40th-day remembrance meeting, Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina offered sympathy to the bereaved family at the residence of Firoz Kabir at Satbariya. While addressing the memorial meeting at Satbariya College ground, Sheikh Hasina encouraged the general people of Pabna to be by the side of late Tafiz Uddin's family. Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina expressed her heartfelt gratitude for Tafiz Uddin's lifelong political allegiance with the Bangladesh Awami League. Ahmed Firoz Kabir, like his father, has remained with the people of Pabna. He has been relentlessly working for the welfare of the mass people of Pabna.

Firoz Kabir got involved with politics from an early age. As his father was a devoted soul and a prominent public leader, many central Awami League leaders frequented their house. From early childhood, he participated in various programs of the Awami League with his father, campaigned in many elections, participated in procession meetings. While studying at Rajshahi College and Rajshahi University, he participated in various programs of Chatra League. He played a crucial and courageous role in the movement against Zia and Ershad. After completing his post-graduate studies from Dhaka University, he returned to his village despite having various opportunities to take up government service. Instead, he followed his father's footsteps and joined the politics.

After his father's death in 1998, the leaders of Sujanagar Upazila and Pabna district Awami League elected Firoz Kabir as a member of the Upazila Awami League. In 2009, he participated in the Sujanagar Upazila Parishad elections by the direction of the Sheikh Hasina. He had a landslide victory that proved the support of the people of Pabna towards his devotion.

While serving as chairman, he established himself as an ideal and honest leader like his father. He went from village to village, trying to organize the Awami League. He built up a close connection with the ordinary people of the Upazila. One of the most striking features of his characteristic qualities is his ability to lead a simple and ascetic lifestyle. He always stands by the people in all their difficulties and challenges and extends a helping hand to them. During the One-Eleven political crisis, he boldly maintained contact with the leader of the people, Sheikh Hasina, and regularly participated in political activities.
